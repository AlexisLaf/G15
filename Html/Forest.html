Forests cover nearly 31% of Earth's land area and are home to over 80% of terrestrial species. They play a crucial role in carbon sequestration, climate regulation, and providing livelihoods. Yet, deforestation and degradation, driven mainly by agricultural expansion, continue at alarming rates. Sustainable forest management is imperative to halt biodiversity loss and combat climate change.

Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon, a vital carbon sink, is under severe threat from deforestation and climate change. Recent studies show that prolonged droughts can transform parts of the rainforest from carbon absorbers to emitters, exacerbating global warming.

Teak Forests

Unsustainable logging practices have led to the decline of wild teak forests, reducing genetic diversity and disrupting ecosystems. Efforts to promote sustainable forestry are essential to preserve these valuable resources.

Indonesian Forests

Indonesia's rainforests, home to unique species like orangutans and Sumatran tigers, are rapidly disappearing due to palm oil plantations and illegal logging. Protecting these forests is critical for biodiversity and climate regulation.
