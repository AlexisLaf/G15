Freshwater ecosystems—rivers, lakes, and aquifers—are vital for biodiversity, agriculture, and human consumption. However, pollution, overuse, and climate change are degrading these resources, threatening both ecological balance and human health. Protecting and restoring freshwater systems is essential for sustainable development and the well-being of all life forms.

Seine

Once heavily polluted, the Seine is now showing signs of ecological recovery. The recent discovery of rare mussels and increased fish diversity reflects the success of restoration efforts. However, ongoing threats like urban runoff and invasive species necessitate continued vigilance to sustain this progress.

Green Lakes

Eutrophication, driven by nutrient pollution and climate change, is turning many lakes into "green" zones dominated by algal blooms. This process diminishes oxygen levels, leading to biodiversity loss and increased greenhouse gas emissions.

Groundwater

Often overlooked, groundwater pollution from industrial waste and agricultural runoff threatens ecosystems and human health. Protecting these hidden water sources is crucial for maintaining biodiversity and ensuring sustainable water supplies.
