Mountains host a significant portion of the world's biodiversity and provide essential resources like freshwater to over half of humanity. They are centers of cultural diversity and support unique ecosystems. However, mountain regions face threats from climate change, deforestation, and unsustainable development. Protecting mountain ecosystems is vital for biodiversity conservation and the services they provide to both local and global communities.

Tibetan Plateau

Known as the "Third Pole," the Tibetan Plateau is experiencing rapid warming, affecting water sources for billions. Protecting this fragile ecosystem is vital for regional and global climate stability.

Kilimanjaro

Kilimanjaro's glaciers are retreating due to climate change, impacting water availability and local biodiversity. Conservation efforts are needed to preserve this iconic mountain and its ecosystems.

Eastern Arc Mountains of Kenya and Tanzania

These ancient mountains are biodiversity hotspots facing deforestation. Protecting them is crucial for preserving endemic species and maintaining ecological balance.
